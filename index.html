📚 Theory of Projectile Motion

Understanding the physics behind the simulation

01

What is Projectile Motion?

Projectile motion is the motion of an object thrown or projected into the air, subject only to the acceleration of gravity. The object is called a projectile, and its path is called its trajectory.

The key insight is that the horizontal and vertical components of motion are independent of each other. Horizontal motion has constant velocity, while vertical motion is uniformly accelerated due to gravity.

02

Key Equations

Horizontal Position
x(t) = v₀ · cos(θ) · t
Vertical Position
y(t) = h₀ + v₀ · sin(θ) · t − ½ · g · t²
Horizontal Velocity
vₓ = v₀ · cos(θ) = constant
Vertical Velocity
vᵧ(t) = v₀ · sin(θ) − g · t
Maximum Height
H = h₀ + (v₀ · sin(θ))² / (2g)
Range (h₀ = 0)
R = v₀² · sin(2θ) / g
Time of Flight (h₀ = 0)
T = 2 · v₀ · sin(θ) / g
03

Assumptions & Conditions

  • ✦
    No Air Resistance — The only force acting on the projectile is gravity.
  • ✦
    Uniform Gravity — Gravitational acceleration (g) is constant throughout the motion.
  • ✦
    Flat Earth Approximation — The ground is assumed to be flat (no curvature effects).
  • ✦
    Point Mass — The projectile is treated as a point mass (no rotation or spin).
  • ✦
    Two-Dimensional Motion — Motion is confined to a vertical plane.
04

Special Cases & Insights

🎯

Maximum Range

For level ground (h₀ = 0), maximum range is achieved at θ = 45°.

🔄

Complementary Angles

Angles θ and 90° − θ give the same range on level ground.

⏱️

Symmetry of Time

Time ascending = Time descending (when launched and landing at same height).

📐

Independence

Horizontal and vertical motions are completely independent of each other.

✏️ Complete Worked Example

Problem: A ball is launched at 50 m/s at an angle of 30° from the ground (h₀ = 0). Find all key values.

1 Break velocity into components
vₓ = v₀ × cos(θ) = 50 × cos(30°) = 50 × 0.866 = 43.30 m/s
vᵧ₀ = v₀ × sin(θ) = 50 × sin(30°) = 50 × 0.500 = 25.00 m/s
2 Find Maximum Height (H)
Formula
H = (v₀ × sin(θ))² / (2 × g)
H = (25.00)² / (2 × 9.8) = 625 / 19.6 = 31.89 m
3 Find Time to Maximum Height
Formula
t_apex = v₀ × sin(θ) / g
t_apex = 25.00 / 9.8 = 2.55 seconds
4 Find Total Time of Flight (T)
Formula (when h₀ = 0)
T = 2 × v₀ × sin(θ) / g
T = 2 × 25.00 / 9.8 = 50 / 9.8 = 5.10 seconds

💡 Notice: T = 2 × t_apex (time going up = time coming down)

5 Find Range (R)
Formula (when h₀ = 0)
R = v₀² × sin(2θ) / g
R = 50² × sin(60°) / 9.8 = 2500 × 0.866 / 9.8 = 220.92 m
6 Find Impact Speed
When h₀ = 0: Impact Speed = Launch Speed = 50.00 m/s

💡 Energy is conserved — it lands at the same speed it was launched!

⚡ Quick Reference & Planet Gravity

Important facts your teacher may ask about:

  • 🎯
    45° = Maximum Range — On flat ground, sin(2×45°) = sin(90°) = 1 (the maximum value).
  • 🔄
    Complementary angles give same range — 30° and 60° give equal range but different heights.
  • ⏱️
    Time up = Time down — When h₀ = 0, the trajectory is perfectly symmetric.
  • ➡️
    Horizontal velocity never changes — No air resistance means vₓ stays constant throughout.
  • ⬆️
    Vertical velocity = 0 at apex — The ball momentarily stops going up before falling.
  • 💥
    Impact speed = Launch speed — When h₀ = 0, energy conservation means it lands at the same speed.

🌍 Gravity on Different Planets

Planetg (m/s²)Effect on Motion
🌍 Earth9.8Standard — our default
🌙 Moon1.62~6× less gravity → goes much higher & farther
🔴 Mars3.72~2.6× less → higher & farther than Earth
🟠 Jupiter24.79~2.5× more → shorter range & lower height
